The Framework

The 5 Behavioral Signals Amazon Tracks at the Query Level

These are not theories. They are confirmed data fields in Amazon's personal data export. Every seller should understand what they mean for listing performance.

Signal 01

Click-Through Rate

Amazon tracks whether shoppers click your listing for every query it appears in. Your main image, title, price, and review count are the entrance filter. Low CTR is logged as failure — per keyword, per session.

Signal 02

Add-to-Cart Rate

A separate field tracks whether clicks convert to add-to-cart. Your detail page — bullets, images, price anchoring, reviews — is the evaluation layer. Getting the click is only the first gate.

Signal 03

Conversion Rate

Purchase is tracked per session, per keyword. This is the terminal signal. Amazon measures the full funnel collapse — from impression to purchase — for every search query on the platform.

Signal 04

Query Abandonment

A boolean field: query_abandoned. Shopper searched, saw your listing, left without clicking anything. Amazon logs this as intent dissatisfaction — not a neutral event. Your listing was part of the reason they left.

Signal 05

Query Reformulation

Shopper changed their search after seeing results that included your listing. They decided what they typed wasn't quite right. You were shown. You were part of why they kept looking. Amazon records this too.
